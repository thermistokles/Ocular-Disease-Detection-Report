Background

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, more than 3.4 million American citizens are either legally blind or have corrected vision.

Approximately 7% of American children under the age of 18 have been diagnosed with an ocular disease. Vision loss is among the top 10 disabilities among the citizens of the US.

The most common causes of vision loss or blindness are cataract, glaucoma, age related macular degeneration(wet and dry), diabetes related retinopathy. Some of these diseases are also hereditary. Diagnosis of these diseases generally require you to get examined by an opthalmist or get multiple tests done.

Procedure

Following procedure was followed for the project:

  • Model Comparison

    We trained the data using different models such as VGG19, ResNet152V2, EfficientNetB0 and EfficientNetB4 to perform better.

  • Upsampling and Downsampling(224 x 224)

    We tried upsampling and downsampling on all the model . However downsampling did not improve the accuracy because of the loss of local features when original image was downscaled. Upsampling could not be implemented on 512 x 512 due to GPU limitations.

  • Pseudo Labeling.

    We performed pseudo labeling using 184 unlabeled images. This increased accuracy by 1%.

Conclusion

  • When predicting classes, image resolution is crucial. Compared to the model trained with 224 x 224 images, the model trained with 512 x 512 images has shown better results. This is due to the loss of local features caused by downscaling from the original image.

  • Pseudo Labeling was done for image size 512 x 512 and applied to EfficientNetB0 which improved the accuracy by 1%.

  • The best model for this project is EfficientNetB0 which achieved an accuracy of 76% and F1 score of 0.75.

  • If random 256x256 slices from the original resolution of the image are taken, stacked, and fed into the convolutional neural network, better results can be obtained.
